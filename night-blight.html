Night Blight

Noctis Rubiginem

Species: Entia Anomala
Scientific Name: Noctis Rubiginem

Night Blights are small, non-hostile anomalous entities primarily found in the central United States. They are capable of burrowing through nearly any terrain, including compact soil, stone, and man-made materials.

Due to their nocturnal nature, Night Blights spend most daylight hours underground in self-made tunnel systems. Their compact musculature and hardened claws allow them to excavate efficiently while remaining hidden from predators.

Despite their anomalous origin, Night Blights pose little threat to humans and have historically acted as natural pest control and soil aerators.
